📖 Signal Guide

Signal Flow Basics

  • Audio flows from source → processing → output
  • Order matters: EQ before compression sounds different than compression before EQ
  • Gain staging: keep levels consistent through the chain to avoid distortion

Common Effects

EQ
Boost/cut frequency ranges — low = bass, mid = body, high = presence/air.
Compressor
Reduces dynamic range. Threshold = where it kicks in · Ratio = how much · Attack/Release = speed.
Distortion / Overdrive
Adds harmonics — warmth to grit depending on amount.
Chorus
Duplicates signal with slight pitch/time variation — thickens sound.
Phaser / Flanger
Comb filtering effects — swooshy/jet sounds.

Typical Signal Chain Order

  1. Tuner
  2. Wah / Filter
  3. Compressor
  4. Overdrive / Distortion
  5. EQ
  6. Modulation (chorus / phaser)
  7. Delay
  8. Reverb
